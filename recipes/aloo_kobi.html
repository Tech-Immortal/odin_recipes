Aloo Kobi

Ingredients

  • 480gms cauliflower cut into small floret
  • 260gms potatoes boiled, peeled and diced to bite size pieces
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Pinch of hing/ asafoetida
  • 1 heaped tsp cumin seeds
  • ½” ginger blended to a paste with a splash of water
  • 3 green chillies slit lengthwise
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 ½ tbsp coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Handful of coriander for garnish finely chopped
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • 1 tbsp dried fenugreek leaves/ kasoori methi (optional)
  • ½” ginger slivers for garnish

Method

  1. In a non stick large heavy bottom saucepan heat the oil over a medium flame.
  2. Add the hing and cumin seeds. Fry for a minute followed by the ginger paste and slit green chillies.
  3. Fry for 20 seconds and add the turmeric and coriander powder frying for a few seconds
  4. Add the cauliflower florets and season to taste.
  5. Stir well over a medium heat for a minute making sure the florets are coated in the spices wellPunjabi Aloo Gobi (4).
  6. Add 50mls water and stir. Turn the heat to a low setting and cook for 15-17 minutes with a lid on.
  7. Make sure to stir halfway through cooking.
  8. Add the boiled potatoes and mix well. Garnish with coriander, lemon juice, kasoori methi and ginger slivers.
  9. Stir well and turn the heat off keep warm with a lid on for a few minutes. Serve warm with chapattis and raita.
